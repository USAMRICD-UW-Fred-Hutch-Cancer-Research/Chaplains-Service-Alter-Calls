Here is a list of historical "standing orders"—long-running, state-sponsored campaigns waged against abstract concepts, social vices, or non-state enemies. Like the modern War on Drugs or War on Terror, these initiatives often featured open-ended mandates, special legal frameworks, and the use of military or police power to enforce moral or political orthodoxy. \[[1](https://filtermag.org/drug-terror-war/)\]

**Modern Era (20th–21st Century)**

* **The War on Drugs (1971–Present)**Officially declared by President Richard Nixon in 1971, who called drug abuse "public enemy number one." However, the legal framework began decades earlier with the **Harrison Narcotics Tax Act of 1914**, which regulated opiates and cocaine. The campaign has spanned over 50 years, costing roughly $1 trillion and leading to the mass incarceration of millions, with agencies like the DEA created specifically to enforce these standing orders.  
* **The War on Terror (2001–Present)**Initiated by President George W. Bush following the September 11 attacks. Unlike traditional wars against nations, this is a global military and legal campaign against the tactic of terrorism and non-state actors (like Al-Qaeda and ISIS). It relies on the **2001 Authorization for Use of Military Force (AUMF)**, which has been used by four successive presidents to justify operations in at least 22 countries, creating a state of "forever war".  
* **The Red Scare / McCarthyism (1947–1957)**A domestic "war" on the ideology of Communism. While not a shooting war, it functioned as a government-wide standing order to root out "subversives" from the State Department, military, and entertainment industry. It was driven by the **House Un-American Activities Committee (HUAC)** and Senator Joseph McCarthy, utilizing blacklists and loyalty oaths to enforce conformity.  
* **The War on Alcohol (Prohibition) (1920–1933)**Codified by the **18th Amendment** and the **Volstead Act**, this was a 13-year federal mandate to ban the manufacture and sale of intoxicating liquors. It was a "moral war" that deployed federal agents to destroy property and arrest citizens for a vice that had previously been legal. It ultimately failed due to the rise of organized crime and public non-compliance. \[[2](https://www.economic-historian.com/p/prohibition-in-the-united-states), [3](https://www.youtube.com/shorts/GPDM2zGup0s), [4](https://www.brennancenter.org/our-work/analysis-opinion/ending-post-911-forever-wars), [5](https://heinonline.org/hol-cgi-bin/get_pdf.cgi?handle=hein.journals/condp11&section=14#:~:text=Prior%20to%20NARA%2C%20the%20last%20major%20federal,public%20record%20so%20that%20taxation%20was%20possible.), [6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_on_drugs), [7](https://americanarchive.org/primary_source_sets/war-on-drugs), [8](https://millercenter.org/the-presidency/educational-resources/age-of-eisenhower/mcarthyism-red-scare), [9](https://coldwar.unc.edu/theme/the-red-scare/)\]

**19th Century**

* **The War on the Slave Trade (1808–1860)**Following the Slave Trade Act of 1807, the British Royal Navy established the **West Africa Squadron** with standing orders to patrol the African coast and suppress the transatlantic slave trade. This was a 50-year naval campaign where British ships effectively acted as an international police force, capturing 1,600 slave ships and freeing 150,000 Africans. The U.S. Navy joined these patrols later, following the Webster-Ashburton Treaty of 1842\.  
* **The War on Vagrancy (14th–19th Century)**England passed a series of laws effectively declaring war on "idleness." The **Vagrancy Act of 1824** (parts of which are still in force in the UK) made it a criminal offense to sleep rough or beg. This was the culmination of centuries of "Poor Laws" dating back to 1349, which viewed homelessness and unemployment not as economic conditions but as moral failings and crimes against the state, punishable by whipping, branding, or hard labor. \[[10](https://www.brookings.edu/articles/governments-greatest-achievements-of-the-past-half-century/), [11](https://www.facebook.com/groups/270764112473385/posts/532098229673304/), [12](https://www.facebook.com/groups/270764112473385/posts/435147799368348/), [13](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vagrancy_Act_1824), [14](https://www.southernafricalitigationcentre.org/wp-content/uploads/2017/08/04_SALC-NoJustice-Report_A-Short-History-of-English-Vagrancy-Laws.pdf)\]

**Early Modern & Medieval (12th–18th Century)**

* **The War on Piracy (17th–18th Century)**During the "Golden Age of Piracy," European powers engaged in a perpetual campaign to eradicate pirates, whom they legally designated *hostis humani generis* ("enemies of all mankind").  
  * **Standing Order:** Governments issued proclamations, such as King George I's **1717 Proclamation for Suppressing of Pirates**, which offered a mix of pardons for surrender and bounties for capture.  
  * **Outcome:** The Royal Navy and colonial courts waged a relentless extermination campaign, hanging pirates in public execution docks (like Execution Dock in London) to serve as a permanent warning.

* **The War on Witches (1450–1750)**A state and church-sponsored campaign to root out "witches" believed to be conspiring with the Devil. This "war" was codified by texts like the *Malleus Maleficarum* (1487) and enforced by secular courts.  
  * **Scope:** It is estimated that 40,000 to 60,000 people were executed across Europe and its colonies (including the Salem Witch Trials in 1692).  
  * **Tactics:** Authorities used special tribunals, torture to extract confessions, and public executions to cleanse society of a perceived spiritual threat.

* **The War on Heresy (The Inquisition) (1184–1834)**Perhaps the longest "standing order" in history, the Inquisition was a permanent institution established by the Catholic Church (and later the Spanish Crown) to combat the crime of heresy (wrong belief).  
  * **The Spanish Inquisition (est. 1478):** A state-controlled bureau initiated by Ferdinand and Isabella to root out secret Jews and Muslims. It lasted over 350 years, utilizing anonymous accusations, secret trials, and *autos-da-fé* (public acts of faith/punishment) to enforce religious unity as a matter of national security.  
  * **The Crusade against the Cathars (1209–1229):** A literal military war declared by the Pope against heretics in Southern France, treating religious dissent as a treasonous insurrection. \[[15](https://digitalcommons.chapman.edu/vocesnovae/vol8/iss1/5/#:~:text=In%20September%201717%2C%20King%20George%20I%20of,those%20individuals%20who%20would%20abandon%20their%20ways.), [16](https://www.ccpl.org/charleston-time-machine/pirate-executions-1718#:~:text=Capital%20offenses%20such%20as%20murder%20and%20piracy,%E2%80%9Cintertidal%20zone%E2%80%9D%20between%20high%20and%20low%20tides.), [17](https://digitalcommons.iwu.edu/cgi/viewcontent.cgi?article=1000&context=history_honproj), [18](https://www.ebsco.com/research-starters/history/spanish-inquisition), [19](https://www.history.com/articles/inquisition), [20](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/how-social-turmoil-has-increased-witch-hunts-throughout-history/), [21](https://www.history.com/articles/beyond-salem-6-lesser-known-witch-trials), [22](https://www.amazon.com/War-Heresy-R-I-Moore/dp/0674065824)\]

**Ancient Era**

* **The Roman War on Brigandage and Piracy**The Roman Empire viewed piracy not just as crime but as a challenge to its sovereignty. In **67 BC**, Rome passed the *Lex Gabinia*, granting the general Pompey extraordinary, open-ended powers (imperium) over the entire Mediterranean Sea to wipe out Cilician pirates. He was given 500 ships and 120,000 troops—an ancient equivalent to a massive, specialized task force targeting a non-state enemy. \[[23](https://www.memoriapress.com/articles/pompey-the-great/#:~:text=As%20his%20fleet%20sailed%20north%2C%20they%20chased,vessels%20in%20the%20Mediterranean%20had%20been%20defeated.), [24](https://medium.com/@JonathanLBell/pompey-and-the-pirates-80877529aaa), [25](https://study.com/academy/lesson/video/mccarthyism-and-the-red-scare-definition-causes-effects.html)\]
