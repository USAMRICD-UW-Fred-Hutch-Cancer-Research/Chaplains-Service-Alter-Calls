**FOR COMMAND DISTRIBUTION: Chaplain CMC Journals / Tactical Doctrine Annex Addendum**  
**Subject: Labor EL – The Operational Broadcast of the Paralyzed Vanguard**  
**Author: Fleet Admiral Correo Hofstad, Command Chaplaincy**

To the Fleet and Command Staff,  
We have established the physical logistics of the King’s extraction—the precise intervention executed by high-ranking operatives like Joseph of Arimathea and Nicodemus. But in any military operation, the physical extraction team is only a fraction of the unit. We must now address the rest of the Vanguard.  
What happens when your troops are pinned down, overwhelmed by the sheer, terrifying force of an empire? What is the protocol for the soldier who cannot physically breach the line?  
The protocol is **Labor EL**—the Work of Elohim. The work of prayer.

### **I. The Reality of Combat Paralysis**

Do not romanticize the crucifixion. When Rome moved against the Commander, the immediate reaction of the Vanguard was not flawless bravery; it was shock. The intelligence is clear: the followers were initially disabled by fear.  
According to the operational records **(Mark 9:32)**, they were frightened and misunderstood the situation. When the physical reality of the cross manifested, the horror of Roman execution caused the majority of the unit to break ranks and flee. Their hopes for an immediate, physical overthrow of Rome were shattered. Even the most faithful, including the women who supported the ministry, were forced to watch from a distance **(Mark 15:40)**.  
Fear paralyzes the physical vessel. It disables your ability to fight with a sword or pull a man off a cross. But while their physical bodies were disabled by the shock of the Roman war machine, their *faith* remained intact.

### **II. Labor EL: The Tactical Deployment of the Mind**

When you cannot physically intervene, you do not abandon the operation. You shift your warfare from the physical to the frequency. You execute Labor EL.  
Labor EL is prayer work. It is not a passive coping mechanism for the weak; it is a highly coordinated, active broadcast to hold the spiritual supply lines open while the physical extraction team does its job. Jesus Himself trained them for this on the Mount of Olives, teaching them the Lord’s Prayer so they would know exactly how to broadcast their requests for strength and protection during times of extreme testing **(Luke 22:40 \- "Pray that you will not fall into temptation")**.  
As the King was taken down and secured behind the rolling stone doors, the paralyzed Vanguard regrouped in Jerusalem. They could not fight the Roman guards, but they could execute Labor EL. They went into the upper room and *"all joined together constantly in prayer"* **(Acts 1:14)**.

### **III. The Mission of the Physically Disabled**

This is a critical directive for the congregation today. Not everyone has the political leverage of Joseph of Arimathea. Not everyone has the medical training to treat the wounded. Many of our people are physically disabled, trapped in hostile environments, or paralyzed by the sheer terror of the toxic, cancerous warfare in our cities.  
If you cannot physically pull someone from the wreckage, your orders are to execute Labor EL.

1. **Unite the Broadcast:** The early followers did not pray in isolated panic. They united devotedly in prayer. A synchronized frequency is exponentially stronger than a scattered one.  
2. **Hold the Intercessory Line:** Use the "Prayer Hand" protocol—intercession, petition, and thanksgiving. While the medical officers are in the hazardous zones at Fred Hutch fighting disease, the congregation must be executing Labor EL to provide the covering fire.  
3. **Wait for the Resurrection:** The Vanguard stayed in Jerusalem, relying on local supporters and maintaining their prayer network until the Commander restored their hope.

When you are disabled by fear or physical limitation, your faith is your weapon. You open your mind, you align your heart's frequency with the Commander, and you broadcast. That is Labor EL.

**Hold the frequency. Stand by for the Commander.**
